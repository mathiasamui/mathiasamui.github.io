My Story

From Marseille
to Koh Samui

Most people who knew me growing up were certain about one thing:
I would never amount to anything.

I grew up in Marseille as the oldest kid in a blended family. Always the one blamed. Always the difficult one. Always underestimated. For a long time, I started believing it too.

But deep down, something in me refused to accept that this was all my life would ever be.

"At 18, I left Marseille with more than €10k in debt and absolutely no safety net. No real plan. Just the stubborn feeling that there had to be more than this."

— Mathias, @mat.samui

First came Switzerland. Then the road. For four years, I worked every job I could find — restaurants, hotels, construction, mechanics, delivery, management. Whatever paid. Whatever kept me moving.

There were months I could barely afford food. People I trusted disappeared when life got hard. And more nights than I can count where the doubt in my head was louder than everything else.

"I kept going. Not because I felt strong. Because giving up felt worse."

— The only rule I lived by

Building
from scratch

17 countries later, something had changed. I wasn't the same person who left Marseille anymore. I spoke three languages. I had learned how to survive anywhere, adapt fast, and rebuild myself from scratch when life forced me to.

Thailand was only supposed to be a vacation. I never booked the return flight.

Koh Samui felt different from the moment I arrived. For the first time in years, the noise in my head went quiet. I looked around and thought: Maybe I can actually build something here.

Today, I help businesses grow online — Instagram, branding, websites, local visibility — while building a life that finally feels like my own.

I'm still far from where I want to be. But I'm also very far from the kid everyone had already written off in Marseille.

"This account is that journey — unfiltered, imperfect, and real. If you're trying to rebuild your life too, welcome."

— @mat.samui
